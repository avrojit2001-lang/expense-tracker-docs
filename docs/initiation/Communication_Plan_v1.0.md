# Communication Plan — Expense Tracker

## Purpose
The communication plan ensures clear stakeholder engagement across the Expense Tracker project lifecycle, with defined channels, frequency, and escalation paths.

## Communications Matrix

| Audience | Message | Owner | Channel | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Product Owner | Scope decisions, approvals | Project Manager | Email, meeting | Weekly |
| Development Team | Sprint tasks, blockers | Scrum Master | Standup, chat | Daily |
| QA Team | Test progress, defects | QA Lead | Dashboard, email | Twice weekly |
| Sponsor | Budget and status | Project Manager | Report | Monthly |
| Users | Feature demonstrations | Product Owner | Demo session | End of sprint |

## Channels
- Email for formal updates and approvals.
- Instant messaging for quick clarifications.
- Video meetings for planning, review, and escalation.
- Shared document repository for versioned artifacts.

## Frequency
- Daily standups for execution alignment.
- Weekly status updates and retrospective preparation.
- Monthly steering committee summaries.
- Sprint review and planning at the end of each sprint.

## Escalation Path
1. Team member raises issue with Scrum Master.
2. Scrum Master notifies Project Manager.
3. Project Manager escalates to Product Owner if scope or timeline risk persists.
4. Product Owner engages Sponsor for budget or high-impact decisions.

## Meeting Cadence
- Daily standup: 15 minutes, team members only.
- Sprint planning: 90 minutes at sprint start.
- Sprint review: 60 minutes at sprint end.
- Retrospective: 45 minutes after each sprint.
- Stakeholder update: 30 minutes weekly.
